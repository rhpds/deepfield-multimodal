= Lab Guide
:toc: left
:toclevels: 3

This is a self-paced lab for Summit Connect attendees.
You will walk through a guided presentation, watch a live classification walkthrough, observe a scale test, and then use the Bootstrap Lab to connect your own data source and build classification agents.

== Prerequisites

* A modern web browser (Chrome, Firefox, Edge, Safari)
* The DeepField Multimodal URL provided by your lab instructor
* No local software installation required -- everything runs in the browser

== Step 1: Click Through the Presentation

When you open the DeepField Multimodal URL, you will see a presentation view.

. Click the *right arrow* or *Next* button to advance through 7 slides
. The slides explain:
** What DeepField Multimodal is -- an agentic signal classification engine
** Why classification matters more than generation for enterprise AI
** The three-tier cascade: nano (deterministic CPU), micro (rule-backed), macro (reasoning)
** The continuous loop: Signals, Decide, Act, Verify, Learn
** The Red Hat + Intel partnership: CPU-native, no GPU required
. Take your time -- each slide has key points about the architecture

After the final slide, you will see a *Start Walkthrough* button.

== Step 2: Step Through the Walkthrough

Click *Start Walkthrough* to begin the auto-orchestrated demo.

The walkthrough tells the story of a factory bearing failure through 8 acts.
Each act runs automatically, then pauses so you can read the results before clicking *Next*.

=== What to watch for in each act:

[cols="1,3"]
|===
| Act | What to Notice

| *The Ordinary World*
| Normal factory metrics: vibration 0.22 RMS, temperature 38.2C. This is the baseline.

| *The Call to Adventure*
| Evidence artifacts arriving one by one. Watch the modality badges -- metric, log, document, image, audio. All feature extraction happens on CPU.

| *Crossing the Threshold*
| The baseline compiler computes normal ranges. Vibration normal: 0.18-0.26. Temperature normal: 37.8-38.5. These become the detection boundaries.

| *Nano Tier*
| Seven deterministic agents classify in sequence. Watch for baseline_distance detecting vibration drift, metric_drift flagging z-scores, log_pattern finding ERROR entries. No LLM. No GPU. Pure CPU.

| *Micro Tier*
| Only escalated evidence reaches here. Image classifier evaluates surface defects. Audio classifier evaluates anomaly scores. Rule-backed, CPU-native.

| *Macro Tier*
| Cross-modal convergence: vibration + temperature + image + audio all pointing to bearing failure. Incident timeline, root cause hypothesis, action plan.

| *The Reward*
| A safe action is proposed: notify maintenance. Requires human approval. Non-destructive by design.

| *The Return*
| A learning proposal: tighten vibration thresholds for earlier detection. Not applied silently -- requires review.
|===

At each pause, click *Next* to advance.

== Step 3: Watch the Scale Run

After the walkthrough, the scale run begins automatically.
This proves the architecture handles real-world volume.

[cols="1,2,2"]
|===
| Scale Act | What Happens | What to Notice

| *10 Lines*
| 10 factory lines, ~60 evidence artifacts
| The nanoagent pipeline scales linearly. Watch the compression ratio and elapsed time.

| *50 Lines*
| 50 factory lines, ~300 artifacts
| Thousands of classifications. Still all on CPU. Watch the compression ratio grow.

| *Stress Test*
| 50 lines at 15% failure rate
| An incident storm. The nano filter absorbs the blast. Macro tier activates for critical findings.

| *Recovery*
| Failure rate drops to 2%
| System stabilizes. Learning proposals capture what was learned from the storm.

| *The Claim*
| Summary statistics
| Total evidence processed, total classifications, peak lines, zero GPU.
|===

== Step 4: Enter the Bootstrap Lab

After the scale run completes, navigate to the *Bootstrap Lab* section.

The Bootstrap Lab is where you connect your own data and build classification agents from scratch.
A frontier model can analyze your data when configured, propose classification rules, and let you validate them before they go live.

The Bootstrap Lab offers four pre-built scenarios.
Pick the one closest to your domain:

| *AAP Jobs*
| IT Operations

To load a scenario:

. Click on the scenario card
. The lab loads sample data from the fixture files
. You will see a preview of the first few samples

== Step 6: Analyze with Quick Start or Deep Analyze

You have two options:

=== Option A: Quick Start (Profile Apply)

If you picked OpenShift Cluster or AAP Jobs, a pre-built profile is available:

. Click *Apply Profile*
. The profile loads pre-built nano rules, taxonomy, and prompts
. No frontier model needed -- instant configuration

Pre-built profiles available:

* `openshift-monitoring` -- Detects CrashLoopBackOff, ImagePullBackOff, NodeNotReady, MemoryPressure, Pending pods, repeated warning events
* `aap-job-health` -- Detects job failures, timeouts, unreachable hosts, workflow failures
* `acs-security` -- Detects critical CVEs (CVSS > 9.0), high CVEs (CVSS > 7.0), policy violations, privileged containers

=== Option B: Deep Analyze (Frontier Model)

For any scenario, you can run a frontier model analysis:

. Click *Analyze*
. Optionally provide hints (e.g., "This is OpenShift pod data" or "These are network metrics")
. The configured frontier model analyzes your sample data
. It returns:
** Detected modality and domain
** Proposed taxonomy (operational states, incident families, action classes)
** Proposed nano rules with conditions and classification outputs
** Suggested micro and macro prompts
. Review the analysis -- you can edit any part before proceeding

== Step 7: Validate -- See the Rubric Matrix

After applying a profile or completing analysis:

. Click *Validate*
. The system runs all proposed agents against the sample data
. You see the *rubric matrix*:

[cols="1,2,2"]
|===
| Color | Meaning | Action

| *Green*
| Accuracy >= 75%, FP rate <= 15%
| Agent is ready for production

| *Yellow*
| Accuracy >= 60%
| Agent works but needs more validation data

| *Red*
| Accuracy < 60%
| Agent needs reconfiguration -- check the rule conditions
|===

The matrix shows each agent with:

* Name and tier (draft/candidate/nano/micro/macro)
* Accuracy, false positive rate, false negative rate
* Coverage (what percentage of evidence it classifies)
* Confidence calibration (none/rough/calibrated)
* Promotion history

== Step 8: Promote Agents

Agents start at the *draft* tier.
To promote an agent:

. Find the agent in the rubric matrix
. Check that it meets the promotion requirements:
** Draft to candidate: 50 samples, 60% accuracy, 30% max FP rate
** Candidate to nano: 200 samples, 75% accuracy, 15% max FP rate, 20% max FN rate
** Nano to micro: 500 samples, 85% accuracy, 10% max FP rate, human reviewed
** Micro to macro: 1,000 samples, 85% accuracy, 5% max FP rate, human reviewed, cross-modal agreement
. Click *Promote*
. The agent's tier advances and its rubric status updates

You can also *demote* agents back to draft if they are not performing well.

NOTE: In the lab environment, sample counts may be lower than the promotion thresholds. The system will still show you the rubric status so you can see where each agent stands.

== Step 9: Explore the Pipeline

After validation, you can test the full pipeline:

. Click *Test Pipeline* to run nano classification against the loaded samples
. See how many classifications are generated
. See which agents fired and what they classified
. Check the baseline confidence

You can also explore the API directly:

[cols="1,2"]
|===
| Endpoint | What It Does

| `GET /api/v1/bootstrap/scenarios`
| List available scenarios

| `POST /api/v1/bootstrap/scenarios/{id}/load`
| Load a scenario's sample data

| `GET /api/v1/bootstrap/profiles`
| List available pre-built profiles

| `POST /api/v1/bootstrap/profiles/{id}/apply`
| Apply a pre-built profile

| `POST /api/v1/bootstrap/analyze`
| Run frontier model analysis on loaded samples

| `POST /api/v1/bootstrap/validate`
| Run validation and get rubric matrix

| `GET /api/v1/bootstrap/rubric`
| Get current rubric matrix

| `POST /api/v1/bootstrap/promote/{agent_id}`
| Promote an agent to the next tier

| `POST /api/v1/bootstrap/test`
| Run nano pipeline against loaded samples
|===

== Step 10: Connect Your Own Prometheus

The ultimate goal: connect DeepField to your own data.

If you have a Prometheus endpoint available:

. In the Bootstrap Lab, select *Prometheus* as the connector type
. Enter your Prometheus URL and any required authentication
. The connector will sample 200 data points
. Run the analysis -- the frontier model will identify your metrics, propose taxonomies, and generate classification rules
. Validate the agents against your real data
. Promote the ones that pass

If you have a Kubernetes/OpenShift cluster:

. Select *Kubernetes* as the connector type
. The connector reads pods, events, and node status from the cluster API
. The analysis proposes rules for your specific cluster's patterns

If you have CSV or log files:

. Select *File* as the connector type
. Point it at your data file
. The connector reads and samples from the file

== Closing

You have now:

. Seen the three-tier classification cascade in action
. Watched it scale to 50 production lines and handle an incident storm
. Used the Bootstrap Lab to connect a data source and build agents
. Validated agents with the rubric matrix and promoted them through tiers

The key takeaway: *enterprise AI starts with classification, not generation*.
The measured proof panel shows whether the current runtime met the 98% CPU pre-expensive target.
Frontier models are used once at bootstrap to generate rules, then the system runs deterministically.

To deploy DeepField Multimodal in your own environment, see the xref:deployment.adoc[Deployment Guide].

To connect your own Prometheus, Kubernetes, or file data sources to a running instance, use the Bootstrap Lab API:

....
POST /api/v1/bootstrap/connect
{
  "source_type": "prometheus",
  "config": {
    "url": "https://your-prometheus:9090",
    "query": "up"
  }
}
....
